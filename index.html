Our Vision

The internet was created by people that connected their networks and allowed traffic from, to and over their network to pass for free. Because everybody did this, there was a huge synergy. The Things Network is doing the same thing for the IoT network of the future.

Connect Networks

The internet was created by people that connected their networks and allowed traffic from, to and over their network to pass for free. Because everybody did this, there was a huge synergy and that is why you are now able to check your Facebook every 5 minutes.

IoT Solution

We are doing the same thing for the IoT network of the future. Internet of Things is demanding a long range, low battery and low cost solution for delivering sensor data and pushing control data between the cloud and devices.

Coverage

With current technologies it is possible to build a community owned low cost data network. A country like The Netherlands reaches complete coverage with less than 80 gateways. A city like Amsterdam only needs 5 gateways at the cost of a laptop.

Building the Network

Our mission is to create abundance in the connectivity for things by crowd sourcing a long range, low battery data network and making it open and free for everyone to use. New network technologies allow for things to talk to the internet without 3G or WiFi. Using little power so their battery will last for 3 years and reaching up to 15 kilometers.

Imagine that things can truly connect to the internet without cumbersome wifi codes or paying for mobile subscriptions. Instead of leaving this task up to larger corporations our mission is to fund the network by it’s users. With a small group of people it is possible to provide an entire city of data connectivity.

In a period of 4 weeks the coverage of the city of Amsterdam was crowd sourced and implemented. From day one use cases were build on top of this network. We believe the abundance we are creating will lead to exponential innovation from which we all benefit.

Use Cases

Use cases range from a low cost position trackers in a shipping container to a small bracelet for you cat so you will always be able to find it.

In 2020 we expect 40 billion connected devices.

Everybody should be able to put sensor data on the ether and fetch the data online. No monopolies or limited player markets should be allowed here.
