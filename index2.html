Unleashing the Internet of Things

We are a group of people on a mission to build a global open free crowd sourced long range low battery data network.

Learn more Contribute

What we do

We crowd sourced a complete internet of things data network with the citizens of the city of Amsterdam in 4 weeks covering the entire city based on a new technology named LoraWAN. Now we are launching our global campaign to repeat this in every city in the world.

The technology allows for things to talk to the internet without 3G or WiFi. So no WiFi codes and no mobile subscriptions. With low battery usage, long range and low bandwidth. Perfect for the internet of things.

Our vision

The internet was created by people that connected their networks and allowed traffic from, to and over their servers and cables to pass for free. Because everybody did this, there was abundant data communication and exponential innovation as a result of that. The Things Network is doing the same thing for the IoT network of the future by creating abundant data connectivity for things.

NEW DATA CONNECTIVITY TECHNOLOGIES

New data network technologies allow for things to connect to the internet without using 3G or WiFi. This technology is called LoraWAN and it used perfect for the internet of things as it is low battery, long range and low bandwidth. Imagine a network that can be used without cumbersome WiFi password, mobile subscriptions and zero setup costs.

Low costs

Because the reach is very high and the costs of the equipment and the devices is low. Covering a complete city can be done with a small investment. The city of Amsterdam was covered with a joined investment of only 10.000 euros.

Crowd sourcing

Because the costs are very low. We do not have to leave the task of buidling such a network up to large telco operators. Instead of that we can crowd source the network and make it available publicly without any form of subscription. Our mission is to build this network by the users for the users.

Building the network

With a small group of people it is possible to provide an entire city of data connectivity for things.
In a period of 4 weeks the coverage of the city of Amsterdam was crowd sourced and implemented.

From day one use cases were build on top of this network. We believe the abundance we are creating will lead to exponential innovation from which we all benefit. Join our global campaign by contributing by making sure your city is next. You can do this by:

1. Help build the network by installing a receiver.
2. Join The Things Network Community as a developer.
3. Become a The Things Network major of your city.

You are the network. Lets build this thing together.

Use cases

Use cases range from bike finders to industrial sensors. Because we use an open standard a lot of devices and things supporting the network technology are already being made.
Partnering with The Things Network the use cases below are being created right now.

Take a look at a couple of use cases down below. Or do you have a great idea yourself?

Boat water detector

Amsterdam has a lot of boat and unfortunately also quite some rain. Boats filling up with water and sinking half way is not an uncommon problem in the city. A water detector was developed that recognizes if your boat is filling up with water. If this happens the owner receives a SMS. When SMS-ing back “clear my boat” a service will come by to remove the water.

I want to ride my bicycle

Lora-wan opens the door to low-energy, low cost applications. We are proud to invite you to crowdfund this project, allowing you to pin-point your bike at any time. It’s a safe and simple device you attach to or place in your bike, with an app that helps you find your bike and get notifications when it’s being moved.More info

Rhino finder in Zimbabwe

Usecase number 1. Description and explanation of this project. Plus a short description why they support TheThings Network.

Peerby

The P2P sharing service Peerby wants to offer it’s users the ability to track their lended goods. With a tracker that is specifically developed for them users will always know the whereabouts of their things and it will also track usage of electronic devices.

WeShareSolar

WeShareSolar is a very innovative concept to enable people to make use of solar power even though they do not own the property to place solar panel. The realtime information of the power consumption of their users and the yield of their solar panels is critical. Because both locations are not guaranteed with WiFI coverage or any other form of internet access. And the data rates are low The Things Network is perfect for them to use.

Port of Amsterdam

The Port of Amsterdam is a wide ranged area. Sensors and detectors are providing real time management information. Because the entire area is too big for WiFi and 3G mobile connectivity is too costly, they embrace The Things Network by placing gateways and they are directly contributing to the public network so everybody can benefit. On the other hand companies surrounding the area are covering blind spots at the edges of the port helping them out in return.

QwikSense

They make the environmental conditions inside of buildings available by placing their special sensors. This information is very valuable for increasing employer comfort and health.

Open

We have written a Manifest that states that this network is build open. Any work done in the community will be open sourced. The foundation that is The Things Network is setup in a decentralised way not giving any point of control or any point of failure that is centralized or owned by the founders. We strongly believe that innovation will florish on this network as long as it is completely open and free. Money can be made on this network only by providing valuable services. We compare the network with the street you walk on. You don’t pay for walking on the pavement, but you pay for the great pizza that's sold on that street. See our open source repositories here.
